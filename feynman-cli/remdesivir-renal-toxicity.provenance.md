## Gaps and Limitations Acknowledged

1. **No long-term follow-up data (>60 days)** — Cannot assess chronic kidney disease progression post-remdesivir
2. **No pharmacogenomic studies** — Cannot identify genetic predictors of nephrotoxicity
3. **No dose-response RCT** — Cannot optimize dosing in severe renal impairment
4. **Limited drug-drug interaction data** — Concomitant nephrotoxins not systematically studied
5. **Human mechanism studies lacking** — Mitochondrial injury in rat I/R model not directly replicated in human kidney tissue

**Mitigation:** Document explicitly identifies these gaps (Section 8) and recommends prospective studies to fill them.

## Consensus and Disagreement Summary

**Consensus:**
- Remdesivir is safe in eGFR <30; no dose adjustment needed
- SBECD excipient and GS-704277 metabolite drive nephrotoxicity, not active GS-441524
- Clinical AKI rates not elevated vs. placebo/untreated controls
- Age >65, male gender, pre-existing CKD associated with higher absolute AKI rates

**Areas of Incomplete Evidence:**
- Long-term renal outcomes (no data >60 days post-treatment)
- Clinical significance of subclinical mitochondrial injury (animal data only)
- Dose-response relationship (standard dose studied; de-escalation not tested)

**No Major Disagreements:** Consensus across RCTs, meta-analyses, and observational cohorts is strong and consistent.

## Recommendations for Future Use

1. **For Clinical Guidelines:** This review provides strong evidence (Level 1) to inform updated FDA/EMA guidance on remdesivir dosing in severe renal impairment. The prior "caution" language should be updated to reflect RCT evidence of safety.

2. **For Patient Counseling:** Clinicians can counsel patients that remdesivir-associated AKI is rare and not increased vs. placebo; close monitoring during treatment is standard practice.

3. **For Future Research:** Prospective longitudinal studies tracking renal outcomes >6 months post-discharge, pharmacogenomic GWAS studies, and dose-optimization RCTs in eGFR <30 populations are recommended.

4. **For Prescribers in Renal Impairment:** No dose adjustment required for standard 5-day remdesivir regimen (200 mg load + 100 mg × 4 days) across all renal function categories, per REDPINE trial (Level 1 evidence).
